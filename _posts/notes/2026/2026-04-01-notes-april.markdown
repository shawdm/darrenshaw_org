## AI

1. Things that make software development easier for AI are the same things that make it easier for
   humans. [Stop Using/init for AGENTS.md](https://addyosmani.com/blog/agents-md/) sounds like advice developers have
   been giving each other for years (don't write comments for things that the code already expresses). Well-structured,
   clean code makes it easier for AI or humans to make changes, or at least understand them. Good tests give AI or
   humans confidence that the system works. APIs (or MCPs) make it easier for humans or AIs to integrate with other
   services.

   We're currently more eager to help AI than we are humans, probably because the payback is so much faster.
2. I've listened to Steve Yegge talk about Gas Town. I didn't understand what he was talking about,
   but [Maggie Appleton's post](https://maggieappleton.com/gastown) did a much better job. My summary
   would be: anthropomorphising coding. I also appreciate the quote, "Newer developers don't have that catalogue of
   failures yet." I am nothing but a catalogue of failures.
3. Benedict Evans is worth reading.
   His [How will OpenAI compete?](https://www.ben-evans.com/benedictevans/2026/2/19/how-will-openai-compete-nkg2x) makes
   a lot of sense, but is all focussed on consumer rather than business adoption of AI. Maybe I'm missing something?
4. Matt Webb always has interesting takes.
   [Headless everything](https://interconnected.org/home/2026/04/18/headless) is one of them.
